These VR Startups are rocking the medical world

One of the most interesting verticals where VR is having big impact is in the medical space. I've always been interested in the medical space; the ability for technology to enhance our wellness never cease to amaze me! Without further ado, here are some startups I have met, what they do, and why it's awesome.

Karuna VR

Opiates are bad. Like, there's kind of an epidemic going on in this country; most recently West Virginia has been in the news because pharmeceutical companies have pushed massive amounts of opiates as treatment, often uncessarily. Well, soon opiates may be a thing of the past, at least as far as the medical field is concerned. Karuna VR is currently in trials in Atlanta and San Francisco to see how effective their prototype is at alleviating pain.

University of Washington Human Interface Technology Laboratory

Pioneered in the 90s, this was one of the very first - and wildly successful - VR apps to treat pain. Their game, SnowWorld, has burn victims who are getting skin grafts, a very painful procedure, throw snowballs at snowmen at an ever-increasing pace. The distraction of the game increases as difficulty ramps up, resulting in up to 90% less pain reported by the patients. Wow!

Studies supporting VR as pain therapy

New Path VR

Focused on end-of-life care, NewPath connects entreprenuers, medical facilities, and doctor groups together for empowring patients and helping them come to terms with their situation, embracing it rather than fighting it. This helps empower patients to be present with their family - and most importantly address their needs around communication and presence.

Aida's Psychology Clinic

Aida's practice helps people in multiple areas, but we'll focus on Immigrant Families. Traditionally she has offered therapy for families to deal with both internal and social issues, and she was inspired to use VR as a medium to help her clients understand what it's like to walk in each others' shoes, and also to educate about best practices when it comes to self care and healing.
